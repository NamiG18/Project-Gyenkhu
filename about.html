Meaning:

Gyenkhu in Dzongkha(the national language of Bhutan) means responsibility. Hence the name; Project Gyenkhu.

Background:

On the 19th of April, 2019, a group of students from Lungtenzampa Middle Secondary School (Thimphu:Butan) with the initiation and guidence of Mr. Eugene Bay, took on the challenge to amalgamate a platform which would tackle the common enemy, PLASTICS. An issue which the students found to be of grave danger to the planet Earth. For that reason, a team named as Project Gyenkhu was formed. Project Gyenkhu is an organization set to minimize the use of plastics and clearing the ignorance of the people. Although we have neglected and failed to cherish the environment, Project Gyenkhu still firmly believes that CHANGE is possible. We are here to sensitize the people and make them mindful of their day-to-day actions. Our main objective is to bring a change in this fast-paced and conservative world of ours.
From holding sessions during the breaks to formal meetings with companies, we certainly have become family more than friends.

Each adversity has fortified us with strength and resilience, each win has brought us joy, and each joy has pulled us closer. What a tremendous journey it has been! Always remember that alone we can do so little but together we can do so much.


Project Gyenkhu is the symbol of HOPE and CHANGE.

Objectives

Project Gyenkhu aims to:

  • Educate the community about the dangers of pollution, climate change and global warming caused by single use PLASTICS
  • Sensitize and advocate the people about various mitigation and measures to minimize the use of non-biodegradable platics.
